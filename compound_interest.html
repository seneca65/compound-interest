💰 Compound Interest

Step 1
Open a Roth IRA with Vanguard
Vanguard is investor-owned — not for profit — which means lower fees and your interests come first. A Roth IRA lets your money grow completely tax-free. Visit investor.vanguard.com to open an account. Fidelity and Charles Schwab are also good options if you already have an account there.
⚠ Important
This is a retirement account — not a savings account
Money in a Roth IRA is meant to stay there until retirement. You cannot withdraw your earnings before age 59½ without paying taxes plus a 10% penalty. Only open one if you are committed to leaving the money alone for the long term.
Step 2
Choose a low-cost ETF
Buy either VTI (Vanguard Total US Stock Market) or VOO (Vanguard S&P 500). Both have an expense ratio of just 0.03% — that means you pay only $3 per year on every $10,000 invested. That tiny fee matters enormously over decades.
Step 3
Set up automatic weekly deposits
Link your checking account and set up an automatic weekly transfer. Even $25 or $50 a week adds up to a surprising amount over time — use the Calculator tab to see for yourself.
Step 4
Nobody is actively trading this — and that's the point
VTI and VOO are index funds. They simply track the market — no one is picking stocks or making trades on your behalf. That simplicity is actually the advantage. Studies show that passive index funds outperform most actively managed funds over the long run.
Step 5
Don't panic when markets dip
Markets go up and down. When they dip, do not sell. Keep making your weekly deposits and let compound interest do the heavy lifting. Time in the market beats timing the market every time.
